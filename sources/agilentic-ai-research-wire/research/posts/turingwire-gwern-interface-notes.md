A research site should reward both skimming and deep reading. Gwern-style pages demonstrate the value of durable long-form notes, visible metadata, and strong typography. TuringWire-style publishing points toward direct headlines, fast orientation, and a clean archive.

## Interface principles

1. **The article is the product.** Navigation should stay quiet once the reader enters an essay.
2. **Metadata should be visible.** Date, status, category, and tags tell readers how to interpret a note.
3. **Search should be local and fast.** A static JSON index is enough for a small publication.
4. **The archive should be browsable.** Tags and categories are not decoration; they are alternate entry points.

The result is a site that can be used as a public lab notebook today and evolve into a more formal publication tomorrow.
